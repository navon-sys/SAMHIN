Our Purpose
Who We Are

South Asian Mental Health Initiative & Network (SAMHIN) is a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization that addresses the mental health needs of the South Asian community in the United States.

Founded in 2013 by Dr. Vasudev Makhija, SAMHIN was born from a recognition that the South Asian community ‚Äî one of the fastest-growing populations in America ‚Äî faced unique barriers to mental health care: cultural stigma, language barriers, lack of culturally aware providers, and a shortage of community-based resources.

Our goal is to decrease the stigma and shame associated with mental illness and to offer free resources for people who seek help ‚Äî including a free provider directory, support groups, educational events, and crisis resources.

Over 3.4 million South Asians live in the United States. SAMHIN is committed to ensuring every one of them has access to the support they deserve.
